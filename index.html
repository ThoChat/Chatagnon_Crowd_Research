Thomas Chatagnon bio

I am currently a Postdoctoral researcher at the Forschungszentrum Jülich (Germany) and part of the Modelling division of the Institute for Advanced Simulation: Civil Safety Research (IAS-7). I am qualified with a PhD in Biomechanics from ENS Rennes (France) and have a background in fluid dynamics (MSc, MSc — Cranfield Uni., UK; ENSEEIHT, France). My research focuses on understanding and modelling the complex dynamics associated with interpersonal contacts in dense human crowds to improve safety and comfort in public spaces. The main research areas associated with my work are human gait and postural control, crowd dynamics, and complex systems modelling.

Research interests: Dense crowd dynamics, biomechanics in crowds, physical interactions.
